designed for a 64x64 grid, so it's easy to access everything. it's assumed you can flip and recolor as needed.

The images are (from top to bottom):
arches and a black section to put behind if you want to hide the next room
nice stone wall and dressed stone columns
a rough boulder wall (or cobblestone floor) and a flagstone floor
the dot is an overlay for floor or wall tiles to create a single dart trap or similar.

"concrete" or a solid, non-descript surface. intended for prototyping or unfinished parts of a dungeon. It also functions as slightly damaged, unlit metal surface.

floor tile grid to give planks or pavers, overlay it on eg. the concrete tile.

water! all the various parts for making a lake in your dungeon.
3x3 trap grid, for spike traps
steel floor tiles (or ceiling)
dirt overlay, put it on the concrete and then the 3x3 tile overlay to create rusty metal tiles.
fancy plaster ceiling or wall panel
2 buttons, one pressed, one not, for use with the boulder wall.
spike trap!
dirt
3x3 tile overlay
grass
the all important teaomatic machine
some more related machine panels

a bunch of ambient occlusion sprites to use as overlays. add AO for free :)
